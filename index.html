Mingalarbar

"Mingalarbar" (pronounces min-ga-lar-bar). Its meaning is depend on the situation. If you say Mingalarbar when you meet someone, it mean "Hello" or "May you be blessed". Sometimes, it means "Bye". Remind that you don't need to stress to say burmese words. My language isn't much difficult but complicated, if you add even only one letter, whole meaning can change. So be careful. In English, the pattern is Subject + Verb + Object, but in Burmese, Subject + Object + Verb. To understand the meaning, you need to listen whole speech haha that's bad. Burmese language has 33 letters and all of them are curved. Like English, Myanmar has many synonym words.

You can also learn from Youtube or google translator. I admit that I sometimes use English-Myanmar google translator but I don't recommend to use the translator. Because if you add only one word or even a letter to the sentence the whole meaning can change. You can rely on the youtube videos but some phrases that use in videos are barely use between Native Speakers. They are more use in writing. I will give you an example, hope you understand.
