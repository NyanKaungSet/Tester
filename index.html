Founder Profile

Yuan Jing

Co-founder and CEO of Shanghai Moonton Technology

Yuan Jing, male, was born on December 26, 1980. Co-founder of Shanghai Moonton Technology Co., Ltd. and currently CEO of Shanghai Moonton Technology Co., Ltd.

In 2003, he graduated from the Department of Computer Science of Tongji University with a bachelor's degree. In the same year, he joined Shengqu Games (formerly Shanda Games) and participated in the project operation of "Legend" and "Bubble Hall". He joined Tencent in 2007 and successively served as the head of operations for "Free Fantasy", "The Legend of Xuanyuan" and "Tribal Guard War", accumulating rich experience in game operation. In 2014, Yuan Jing founded Shanghai Moonton Technology Co., Ltd. as a co-founder. In 2015, Magic Rush: Heroes was launched globally (except mainland China). 2016 Mobile Legends: Bang Bang is available worldwide (except Mainland China). In April 2017, Moonton Technology actively communicated with the Indonesian government and the local e-sports association, which led to the birth of the Indonesian Mobile Legends e-sports professional league (full name Mobile Legends: Bang Bang Professional League, English abbreviation MPL). In 2019, the mobile game Mobile Legends: Bang Bang independently developed by Moonton Technology was recognized by the International Olympic Committee and became a medal event in the Southeast Asian Games. In 2019, Moonton Technology ranked in the top ten of App Annie's 2019 annual revenue list of Chinese game overseas manufacturers. In 2020, Moonton Technology won the honorary title of "2019-2020 National Key Cultural Export Enterprise" jointly issued by the Ministry of Commerce, the Central Propaganda Department, the Ministry of Finance, the Ministry of Culture and Tourism, and the State Administration of Radio, Film and Television. In 2020, Moonton Technology was awarded the "2020 BrandZ Top 50 Chinese Global Brands" jointly released by Google, WPP and Kantar, ranking 24th. In 2020, Moonton Technology won the "Cultural Contribution Award" and "Technology Contribution Award" for the corporate social responsibility of People.com Games. In 2021, Moonton Technology will be selected as the "National Cultural Export Key Enterprise" for two consecutive years, and Mobile Legends: Bang Bang will be selected as the "2021-2022 National Cultural Export Key Project".
In 2021, Moonton Technology and ByteDance reached a strategic acquisition agreement, Moonton Technology joined ByteDance and maintained independent operations, and Yuan Jing continued to lead the company's development as CEO.

Xu Zhenhua

Founder of Shanghai Moonton Technology

Xu Zhenhua, male, was born on January 24, 1979 in Shanghai. The founder of Shanghai Moonton Technology Co., Ltd. is currently the CEO of Shanghai Moonton Technology.

After graduating from university in 2000, he joined the Shanghai Institute of Computing Technology. In 2002, it officially entered the game industry. In 2008, he joined Tencent to engage in the development and operation of online games, and successively participated in the research and development of projects such as "Free Fantasy" and "The Legend of Xuanyuan". In 2012, he launched the "Tribal Guard War" series of games as a producer. In 2014, he founded Shanghai Moonton Technology Co., Ltd. and served as CEO. In 2015, the team creatively developed Magic Rush: Heroes, a mobile game integrating card, tower defense and strategy. In 2016, a mobile MOBA project team was formed to develop Mobile Legends: Bang Bang (MLBB for short). In 2019, the mobile game Mobile Legends: Bang Bang independently developed by Moonton Technology was recognized by the International Olympic Committee and became a medal event in the Southeast Asian Games. In 2019, Moonton Technology ranked in the top ten of App Annie's 2019 annual revenue list of Chinese game overseas manufacturers. In 2020, Moonton Technology won the honorary title of "2019-2020 National Key Cultural Export Enterprise" jointly issued by the Ministry of Commerce, the Central Propaganda Department, the Ministry of Finance, the Ministry of Culture and Tourism, and the State Administration of Radio, Film and Television. In 2020, Moonton Technology entered the "BrandZ China Top 50 Global Brands in 2020" jointly released by Google, WPP and Kantar, ranking 24th. In 2020, Moonton Technology was awarded the "Cultural Contribution Award" and "Technology Contribution Award" for the corporate social responsibility of games by People.com.cn.
